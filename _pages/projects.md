## Workshop Project

We will carry out a collective project in the  Humanities Data and Mapping Environments workshop. The theme of the 2026 project is "Portes de Besançon" (Doors of Besançon). Participants can also work on their own projects. 

_Details will be added here as the workshop progresses._

### Project Learning Outcomes 

Participants will work on a hands-on mapping project throughout the workshop. Projects will:

- Create data relevant to the humanities with a spatial dimension
- Provide an opportunity for critical reflection on data creation 
- Focus on the importance of contextualization and tagging.
- Practice the skill of in situ mapping. 
- Employ one or more digital mapping tools introduced during the workshop
- Be accompanied by brief documentation

### Tips on geotagging images

There are two basic steps for creating an image dataset with a smartphone that is geotagged (= each photo has a latitude/longitude pair associated with it). Each step depends on the phone that you have. 

1. Permissions for the camera: location services have to be enabled for the camera. This will allow you to have geotagged images in your own phone. This step is relatively straightforward and you can find instructions easily for this that are device specific. 
2. Permissions for sharing: you have to allow for images to be shared without the location data being removed. This step is a bit trickier. 

Here are some tips from the various 2026 participants: 

- Don't take the picture within social media and download it.
- Don't send the picture to yourself over other platforms. They often scrape location data from them (and harvest it as a "business opportunity") and remove it from the photos. 
- Use a USB cable for a direct download of pictures from smartphone to laptop.
